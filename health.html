The Problem

In India air pollution causes millions of deaths annually. 15% of children suffer from asthma and other lung diseases. On average, just breathing the air in Delhi is equivalent to smoking 50 cigarettes a day. Still, the government and media are doing almost nothing to combat pollution. Indians are frustrated with the lack of information and the high amounts of pollution they breathe.

Thatâ€™s why our team is working on a mobile website and app called Pavan, which means air in Hindi. We want Indians to be able to check pollution levels like you check weather on your phone. Our platform will let users track air quality and access easy-to-understand information about pollution. It will also allow people to buy air filters and pollution masks. We will generate revenue from sales of these items and from companies wishing to advertise on our platforms. Pavan will empower Indians to take charge of their own health and breathe cleaner air.
